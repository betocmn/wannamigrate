{% blocktrans %} Step-by-Step to Canada {% endblocktrans %}
{% blocktrans %} Canada receives around 250 thousand new immigrants every year. It is a rich and big country with a small population, so immigration is very well organized and encouraged. {% endblocktrans %}
{% trans "Download our e-book" %}: {% trans "Guide - How to move to Canada" %}.
{% trans "1 - Define your goal:" %}
{% blocktrans %} First of all you need to decide what you want to do in Canada:
  • To work and live permanently
  • To study abroad
  • To work temporarily
  • To do business
{% endblocktrans %}
{% trans "2 - Identify the appropriate visa type for your situation:" %}
{% blocktrans %} If you want to live permanently in Canada you have the following options:
  • Skilled Immigrants
  • Skilled Immigrants (Quebec only)
  • Caregivers
  • Business (Self-Employed, Start-up Visa or Investors)
  • Family sponsorship
  • Provincial nominees
If you are going to study in Canada you will need a study permit. You may be able to get the permit to work 20 hours per week.

If you need to work or do business temporarily, you will just need to apply for a temporary visa which lets you do your work while in Canada. {% endblocktrans %}
{% trans "3 - Get the documents to prove your education degree:" %}
{% blocktrans %} To validate you education degree in Canada you need to do an ECA â€“ Education Credential Assessment.

This will demonstrate that your studies are equivalent and recognized in Canada.

You will need your academic records and certifications. These need to be translated to English or French. {% endblocktrans %}
{% trans "4 - Get your foreign language certificate:" %}
{% blocktrans %} Unless you are going as a student to learn English or French, you will need to prove your skills in one of the two languages.

This is done by providing a certificate with your test results for one of the following exams:
  • Celpip (English)
  • IELTS (English)
  • TEF (French)
{% endblocktrans %}
{% trans "5 - Get the documents to prove your work experience:" %}
{% blocktrans %} In some immigration applications you will need to prove your work experience time.

This is done by providing official letters from the companies you worked for, which should have the period you worked there, your exact occupation, duties and a full description.

Each letter should also have the contact details of the company and the person who wrote it. {% endblocktrans %}
{% trans "6 - Get other documents:" %}
{% blocktrans %} Additionally you will need:
  • Passport & Photos
  • Spouse or De-Facto Relationship proofs (if any)
  • Proof of funds (bank statements, etc...)
  • Medical and Police certificates
All documents need to be translated to either English or French. {% endblocktrans %}
{% trans "7 - Make your visa application:" %}
{% blocktrans %} Once you have all the above complete, you can send your application.

Most of the applications can be done online or by paper.

Start your application: http://www.cic.gc.ca/english/immigrate/apply.asp {% endblocktrans %}
